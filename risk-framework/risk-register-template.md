# Cyber Risk Register Template

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Register serves as the central record for identifying, tracking, and managing cybersecurity risks across the organization.

The register ensures that cyber risks are documented, assigned ownership, and monitored until mitigation or formal acceptance occurs.

---

## Risk Register Structure

Each risk entry should include the following information.

| Field | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Risk ID | Unique identifier for the risk |
| System / Asset | System, platform, or service affected |
| Risk Description | Description of the security risk |
| Likelihood | Probability the risk may occur |
| Impact | Potential business impact |
| Risk Score | Likelihood × Impact |
| Risk Owner | Individual responsible for addressing the risk |
| Risk Decision | Mitigate, Accept, Transfer, or Avoid |
| Mitigation Plan | Planned remediation steps |
| Review Date | Date the risk should be reassessed |
| Status | Open, Mitigated, Accepted, Closed |

## Governance Integration

The Cyber Risk Register supports several governance activities:

• Risk committee reviews  
• Security leadership reporting  
• Audit evidence for risk management processes  
• Enterprise risk visibility

Maintaining a centralized register ensures that cybersecurity risks are consistently tracked and managed across the organization.
