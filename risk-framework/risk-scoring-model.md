# Cyber Risk Scoring Model

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Scoring Model provides a consistent method for evaluating and prioritizing cybersecurity risks across the organization.

A standardized scoring approach ensures that risks are assessed objectively and that remediation efforts focus on issues with the greatest potential business impact.

---

## Risk Evaluation Methodology

Cyber risks are evaluated based on two primary factors:

• Likelihood  
• Impact  

These values are combined to determine an overall risk score.

---

## Likelihood Scale

Likelihood represents the probability that a threat could successfully exploit a vulnerability.

| Score | Likelihood |
|------|------------|
| 1 | Rare |
| 2 | Unlikely |
| 3 | Possible |
| 4 | Likely |
| 5 | Almost Certain |

---

## Impact Scale

Impact represents the potential consequence to the organization if the risk were realized.

| Score | Impact |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Minimal operational disruption |
| 2 | Limited operational impact |
| 3 | Moderate operational disruption |
| 4 | Significant service impact |
| 5 | Severe business impact |

---

## Risk Score Calculation

Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact

Example:

Likelihood: 4  
Impact: 5  

Risk Score: 20

---

## Risk Severity Levels

| Score Range | Severity |
|-------------|----------|
| 1–5 | Low |
| 6–10 | Moderate |
| 11–15 | High |
| 16–25 | Critical |

---

## Governance Integration

Risk scores determine how risks are handled within the cyber risk governance process.

Higher severity risks may require:

• Escalation to security leadership  
• Risk committee review  
• Formal risk acceptance documentation  
• Executive reporting
