# Cyber Risk Lifecycle

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Lifecycle defines the process used to identify, evaluate, manage, and monitor cybersecurity risks across the organization.

A structured lifecycle ensures that cyber risks are consistently documented, evaluated using a defined methodology, and managed through mitigation or formal risk acceptance.

---

## Risk Lifecycle Stages

Cyber risks typically move through the following lifecycle stages.

### Risk Identification

Cyber risks are identified through various sources including:

• Vulnerability management findings  
• Security assessments  
• Threat intelligence analysis  
• Incident investigations  
• Internal audits or compliance reviews  

Identified risks should be documented in the enterprise risk register.

---

### Risk Assessment

Once identified, risks are evaluated using the organization's risk scoring methodology.

Assessment factors typically include:

• Likelihood of exploitation  
• Potential business impact  
• Existing security controls  

The result of this evaluation produces a risk score used to determine severity and prioritization.

---

### Risk Prioritization

Risks are prioritized based on severity and potential impact to the organization.

Typical prioritization categories include:

• Low Risk  
• Moderate Risk  
• High Risk  
• Critical Risk  

Higher severity risks require more immediate remediation or governance review.

---

### Risk Treatment

Once evaluated, a risk owner determines the appropriate treatment strategy.

Common risk treatment options include:

| Decision | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Mitigate | Implement security controls to reduce risk |
| Accept | Formally accept the risk with documented approval |
| Transfer | Shift risk through insurance or contractual agreements |
| Avoid | Eliminate the activity creating the risk |

Risk treatment decisions should be documented and tracked in the enterprise risk register.

---

### Risk Monitoring

Cyber risks must be continuously monitored to ensure mitigation activities are progressing and accepted risks remain valid.

Monitoring activities include:

• Periodic risk review cycles  
• Risk committee oversight  
• Executive risk reporting  

Risks should remain active in the register until remediation or closure.

---

## Lifecycle Integration

The Cyber Risk Lifecycle integrates with several components of the cyber risk management program.

These include:

• Risk scoring model  
• Enterprise risk register  
• Risk ownership model  
• Risk acceptance process  
• Executive risk reporting

Together these components ensure that cybersecurity risks are consistently managed throughout their lifecycle.
