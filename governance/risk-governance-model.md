# Cyber Risk Governance Model

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Governance Model defines how cyber risk decisions are evaluated, approved, and monitored across the organization.

A structured governance model ensures that cyber risks are consistently assessed, ownership is clearly defined, and leadership maintains visibility into enterprise risk exposure.

---

## Governance Structure

Cyber risk governance typically operates across three levels.

### Operational Level

Security engineers, platform teams, and system owners identify and document cyber risks during day-to-day operations.

Responsibilities include:

• Identifying vulnerabilities and control gaps  
• Documenting risks in the enterprise risk register  
• Implementing mitigation controls where possible  

---

### Security Risk Management

The security risk function evaluates identified risks and ensures consistent risk scoring and documentation.

Responsibilities include:

• Risk scoring and prioritization  
• Risk analysis and impact assessment  
• Maintaining the enterprise cyber risk register  
• Escalating significant risks for leadership review  

---

### Executive Oversight

Senior leadership provides oversight for significant cyber risk decisions.

Responsibilities include:

• Reviewing high-impact cyber risks  
• Approving risk acceptance decisions  
• Ensuring alignment with organizational risk tolerance  
• Providing accountability for enterprise risk exposure  

---

## Risk Escalation

Cyber risks are escalated based on severity and potential business impact.

Typical escalation triggers include:

• High or critical risk scores  
• Regulatory or compliance implications  
• Risks affecting customer data or critical infrastructure  
• Risks requiring formal acceptance by leadership  

---

## Governance Outcomes

An effective cyber risk governance model ensures:

• Consistent risk evaluation across the organization  
• Clear ownership for risk decisions  
• Executive visibility into cyber risk exposure  
• Accountability for accepted risks  
• Alignment between security operations and business leadership
