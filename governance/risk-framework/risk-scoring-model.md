# Cyber Risk Scoring Model

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Scoring Model provides a consistent method for evaluating and prioritizing cybersecurity risks across the organization.

A standardized scoring approach ensures that risks are assessed objectively and that remediation efforts are focused on the issues with the greatest potential business impact.

---

## Risk Evaluation Methodology

Cyber risks are evaluated based on two primary factors:

• Likelihood  
• Impact

These values are combined to determine an overall risk score.

---

## Likelihood Scale

Likelihood represents the probability that a threat could successfully exploit a vulnerability.

| Score | Likelihood Description |
|------|------------------------|
| 1 | Rare – Highly unlikely to occur |
| 2 | Unlikely – Possible but not expected |
| 3 | Possible – Could occur under certain conditions |
| 4 | Likely – Expected to occur in many situations |
| 5 | Almost Certain – Expected to occur frequently |

---

## Impact Scale

Impact represents the potential consequence to the organization if the risk were realized.

| Score | Impact Description |
|------|--------------------|
| 1 | Minimal operational disruption |
| 2 | Limited operational impact |
| 3 | Moderate service or operational disruption |
| 4 | Significant impact to operations or customer services |
| 5 | Severe impact including financial loss, regulatory exposure, or major service disruption |

---

## Risk Score Calculation

Risk score is calculated by multiplying likelihood and impact.
