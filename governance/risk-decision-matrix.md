# Cyber Risk Decision Authority Matrix

## Purpose

The Risk Decision Authority Matrix defines who has the authority to approve cybersecurity risk decisions based on risk severity.

Establishing clear decision authority ensures that risk acceptance, mitigation prioritization, and escalation decisions are handled consistently and with appropriate leadership oversight.

---

## Risk Severity Levels

Cyber risks are categorized using the organization’s defined risk scoring methodology.

Severity levels include:

• Low  
• Moderate  
• High  
• Critical  

Each level corresponds to a defined approval authority.

---

## Risk Decision Authority

| Risk Severity | Decision Authority | Typical Action |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Low | Risk Owner | Document risk and manage locally |
| Moderate | Security Manager | Mitigation plan required |
| High | Director of Security or Security Leadership | Formal mitigation plan and leadership review |
| Critical | Cyber Risk Committee or Executive Leadership | Risk committee review and executive oversight |

---

## Risk Escalation

Cyber risks should be escalated when:

• Risk scores exceed defined thresholds  
• The risk impacts critical systems or sensitive data  
• Regulatory or compliance obligations may be affected  
• The risk requires formal acceptance beyond operational authority

Escalation ensures that leadership maintains visibility into significant cyber risks.

---

## Governance Integration

The Risk Decision Authority Matrix supports several components of the cyber risk management program:

• Risk scoring model  
• Risk acceptance process  
• Cyber risk governance structure  
• Cyber risk committee oversight

Together these components ensure that cybersecurity risks are evaluated, approved, and monitored with appropriate accountability.
