# Cyber Risk Committee Charter

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Committee provides governance oversight for cybersecurity risks that require leadership awareness, cross-functional coordination, or formal risk acceptance.

The committee ensures that significant cyber risks are reviewed consistently, ownership is clearly defined, and risk decisions align with the organization's risk tolerance.

---

## Scope

The Cyber Risk Committee reviews and provides oversight for:

• High and critical cybersecurity risks  
• Risk acceptance decisions that exceed operational authority  
• Significant security control gaps  
• Emerging threats impacting enterprise systems  
• Security risks with potential regulatory or compliance implications  

---

## Objectives

The objectives of the Cyber Risk Committee include:

• Ensuring leadership visibility into enterprise cyber risks  
• Supporting consistent risk decision-making across the organization  
• Providing accountability for risk ownership and mitigation activities  
• Aligning cybersecurity risk management with business priorities  

---

## Committee Membership

Typical membership may include representatives from the following functions:

• Chief Information Security Officer  
• Security Risk Management  
• IT Infrastructure Leadership  
• Engineering or Platform Leadership  
• Compliance or Legal Representatives  
• Business Unit Leadership (as required)

Additional stakeholders may be invited depending on the nature of the risk under review.

---

## Responsibilities

The Cyber Risk Committee is responsible for:

• Reviewing high-impact cybersecurity risks  
• Approving formal risk acceptance decisions when required  
• Providing guidance on risk mitigation priorities  
• Ensuring accountability for risk remediation activities  
• Monitoring trends in enterprise cyber risk exposure  

---

## Meeting Cadence

The committee typically meets on a recurring basis to review significant risks and program updates.

Recommended cadence:

• Monthly governance review meetings  
• Ad hoc meetings for urgent or critical risk decisions  

---

## Decision Authority

Risk decisions are based on the organization’s defined risk tolerance and risk scoring model.

The committee may:

• Approve risk mitigation plans  
• Approve time-bound risk acceptance  
• Escalate risks to executive leadership when necessary  

---

## Reporting

Key outcomes from committee meetings should be documented and communicated to security leadership and relevant stakeholders.

Committee reporting may include:

• Status of high-severity cyber risks  
• Progress on mitigation initiatives  
• Risk acceptance approvals  
• Emerging cyber threat trends  

This reporting helps maintain transparency and accountability for enterprise cybersecurity risk management.
