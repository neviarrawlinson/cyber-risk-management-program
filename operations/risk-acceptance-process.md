# Cyber Risk Acceptance Process

## Purpose

The Risk Acceptance Process defines how cybersecurity risks may be formally accepted when mitigation is not immediately feasible or when the organization determines that the business need outweighs the associated risk.

A structured process ensures that risk acceptance decisions are documented, reviewed by appropriate stakeholders, and monitored over time.

---

## When Risk Acceptance May Be Considered

Risk acceptance may be appropriate in situations such as:

• Legacy systems that cannot be immediately upgraded  
• Operational constraints that delay remediation  
• Temporary compensating controls are in place  
• The business impact of remediation exceeds the risk exposure  

Risk acceptance should never be used as a substitute for proper risk mitigation when feasible.

---

## Risk Acceptance Workflow

The risk acceptance process typically follows these steps:

1. Risk Identification  
A cybersecurity risk is identified through vulnerability management, security assessments, incident analysis, or internal reviews.

2. Risk Evaluation  
The risk is evaluated using the organization’s risk scoring methodology to determine severity.

3. Risk Documentation  
The risk is documented in the enterprise risk register including system details, impact analysis, and mitigation options.

4. Risk Owner Review  
The designated risk owner evaluates the risk and determines whether mitigation, transfer, avoidance, or acceptance is appropriate.

5. Security Review  
The security risk management function reviews the risk assessment and validates scoring and mitigation options.

6. Approval Decision  
Depending on risk severity, the risk acceptance request may require approval from security leadership or the cyber risk governance committee.

7. Documentation and Tracking  
Approved risk acceptance decisions are recorded in the risk register with a defined review date.

---

## Risk Acceptance Duration

Risk acceptance decisions should be time-bound.

Accepted risks should include:

• A defined expiration date  
• Planned remediation milestones if applicable  
• Periodic review by the risk owner and security team

---

## Governance Oversight

High-impact risk acceptance decisions should be reviewed by security leadership or a formal risk governance body.

This ensures that:

• Leadership is aware of significant cyber risks  
• Risk acceptance aligns with organizational risk tolerance  
• Accepted risks are monitored over time

---

## Continuous Monitoring

Accepted risks must be periodically reviewed to ensure that conditions have not changed and that mitigation options remain appropriate.

Regular review cycles help organizations avoid carrying unnecessary risk for extended periods.
