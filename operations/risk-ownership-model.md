# Cyber Risk Ownership Model

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Ownership Model defines how accountability for cybersecurity risks is assigned across the organization.

Clear ownership ensures that risks are not only identified but also managed, mitigated, or formally accepted by the appropriate stakeholders.

Without defined ownership, cybersecurity risks can remain unresolved and visibility into risk exposure becomes limited.

---

## Key Roles in Cyber Risk Management

### Risk Owner

The Risk Owner is the individual responsible for managing the identified risk.

Typically, the risk owner is the business or technology leader responsible for the system, application, or process where the risk exists.

Responsibilities include:

• Understanding the nature of the risk  
• Coordinating mitigation activities  
• Ensuring risks are tracked in the risk register  
• Providing updates during risk reviews  

---

### Security Risk Advisor

The Security Risk Advisor provides subject matter expertise and guidance regarding cybersecurity risks.

Responsibilities include:

• Performing risk analysis  
• Validating risk scoring  
• Advising on mitigation strategies  
• Escalating risks when necessary  

---

### Risk Decision Authority

Risk Decision Authorities are responsible for approving major risk decisions, including formal risk acceptance.

Responsibilities include:

• Reviewing high-impact cyber risks  
• Approving risk acceptance decisions  
• Ensuring alignment with organizational risk tolerance  

Depending on severity, this role may include:

• Security leadership  
• Technology leadership  
• Executive risk committees  

---

## Shared Responsibility Model

Cyber risk management is a shared responsibility across security, technology, and business leadership.

| Role | Responsibility |
|-----|----------------|
| Business Leaders | Own risks within their systems and processes |
| Security Team | Identify, assess, and advise on cyber risks |
| Engineering / IT | Implement mitigation controls |
| Executive Leadership | Provide oversight for major risk decisions |

---

## Governance Integration

The Risk Ownership Model works together with other components of the cyber risk program including:

• Risk scoring methodology  
• Enterprise risk register  
• Risk governance structure  
• Risk acceptance process  

This integration ensures that cyber risks are consistently identified, evaluated, assigned ownership, and monitored until resolution.
