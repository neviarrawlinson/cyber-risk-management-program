# Executive Cyber Risk Dashboard

## Purpose

The Executive Cyber Risk Dashboard provides leadership with a clear, concise view of the organization’s cybersecurity risk posture.

The goal is to translate technical security data into business-focused insights that support informed decision making.

---

## Key Risk Metrics

The dashboard focuses on a small set of meaningful indicators that reflect the organization’s cyber risk exposure.

### Enterprise Risk Overview

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Total Open Cyber Risks | Number of currently tracked risks |
| Critical Risks | Risks with the highest severity requiring immediate attention |
| High Risks | Risks requiring mitigation planning |
| Accepted Risks | Risks formally accepted by leadership |

---

### Risk Trend Analysis

Leadership should be able to see whether risk exposure is improving or increasing over time.

Example trend indicators include:

• Total risk count over time  
• Critical risk reduction progress  
• Risk remediation cycle time

### Risk Ownership Visibility

Understanding who owns risk is critical for accountability.

Example breakdown:

• Risks by business unit  
• Risks by technology domain  
• Risks by responsible owner  

---

## Executive Reporting Principles

An effective cyber risk dashboard should:

• Focus on business impact rather than technical details  
• Highlight trends rather than isolated metrics  
• Provide clear ownership for each risk  
• Enable leadership to prioritize mitigation efforts

---

## Governance Integration

The Executive Cyber Risk Dashboard is supported by data from:

• The enterprise risk register  
• Risk scoring methodology  
• Risk acceptance documentation  
• Security assessment findings

These inputs ensure leadership has accurate visibility into the organization’s cyber risk posture.
