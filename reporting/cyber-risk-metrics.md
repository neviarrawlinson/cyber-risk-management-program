# Cyber Risk Metrics and Key Performance Indicators

## Purpose

Cyber risk metrics help organizations measure the effectiveness of their cybersecurity risk management program.

Effective metrics provide leadership with visibility into risk exposure, remediation progress, and the overall health of the organization's security posture.

---

## Core Cyber Risk Metrics

### Open Cyber Risks

Tracks the total number of active risks documented in the enterprise risk register.

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Total Open Risks | Number of currently active cyber risks |
| Critical Risks | Risks requiring immediate remediation |
| High Risks | Risks requiring mitigation planning |
| Accepted Risks | Risks formally accepted by leadership |

Monitoring the number and severity of risks helps leadership understand the organization's exposure to cyber threats.

---

### Risk Remediation Metrics

Measures how effectively the organization mitigates identified risks.

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Mean Time to Remediate (MTTR) | Average time required to mitigate a risk |
| Risks Mitigated per Quarter | Number of risks resolved during the reporting period |
| Risk Backlog | Number of risks awaiting remediation |

These metrics help identify bottlenecks in remediation processes.

---

### Risk Ownership Metrics

Ensures accountability across teams responsible for risk mitigation.

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Risks by Business Unit | Distribution of risks across departments |
| Risks by System Owner | Risks assigned to responsible owners |
| Risks Without Owner | Risks that require ownership assignment |

Clear ownership is critical for maintaining accountability in risk management.

---

### Risk Acceptance Metrics

Provides visibility into how often risks are formally accepted rather than mitigated.

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Total Accepted Risks | Risks formally approved for acceptance |
| Accepted Risk Duration | Length of time risks remain accepted |
| Accepted Risks by Severity | Distribution of accepted risks by severity level |

Tracking accepted risks helps ensure risk acceptance is used appropriately and reviewed regularly.

---

### Risk Trend Metrics

Analyzes changes in cyber risk exposure over time.

| Metric | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Risk Trend Over Time | Changes in total risk exposure |
| Critical Risk Reduction | Progress in reducing critical risks |
| Risk Aging | How long risks remain unresolved |

Trend analysis helps leadership determine whether the organization's risk posture is improving.

---

## Executive Reporting

Cyber risk metrics should be summarized and presented through executive dashboards or governance reporting.

Key executive insights include:

- Current enterprise cyber risk posture
- Top critical risks
- Risk remediation progress
- Trends in cyber risk exposure
- Areas requiring leadership attention

---

## Continuous Improvement

Cyber risk metrics should evolve as the organization's security program matures.

Regular review of risk metrics helps organizations improve governance processes, prioritize mitigation efforts, and align cybersecurity risk management with business objectives.
