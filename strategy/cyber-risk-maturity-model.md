# Cyber Risk Management Maturity Model

## Purpose

The Cyber Risk Maturity Model provides a structured way to evaluate the effectiveness and maturity of an organization's cyber risk management program.

It helps security leaders understand the current state of risk management capabilities and identify opportunities for improvement.

---

## Maturity Levels

Cyber risk programs typically evolve through several stages of maturity.

### Level 1 — Initial

Risk management activities are largely reactive and inconsistent.

Characteristics:

- Risks identified informally
- No centralized risk register
- Limited governance oversight
- Risk decisions handled ad hoc

---

### Level 2 — Developing

Basic cyber risk management processes begin to emerge.

Characteristics:

- Risk register established
- Initial risk scoring methodology defined
- Security team tracks risks
- Limited executive reporting

---

### Level 3 — Defined

Cyber risk processes are documented and consistently applied across the organization.

Characteristics:

- Formal risk governance structure
- Defined risk ownership model
- Risk lifecycle processes implemented
- Risk acceptance process documented
- Regular risk reporting to leadership

---

### Level 4 — Managed

Cyber risk management becomes integrated with business decision making.

Characteristics:

- Enterprise-wide risk visibility
- Executive cyber risk dashboards
- Risk committee governance
- Risk metrics and KPIs tracked
- Cross-functional risk ownership

---

### Level 5 — Optimized

Cyber risk management is proactive, data-driven, and continuously improving.

Characteristics:

- Predictive risk analysis
- Automated risk monitoring
- Integrated security telemetry
- Strategic risk planning
- Continuous improvement of governance processes

---

## Using the Maturity Model

Organizations can use this maturity model to:

- Assess the current state of their cyber risk program
- Identify gaps in governance and risk processes
- Prioritize improvements
- Communicate program maturity to executive leadership

---

## Continuous Improvement

Cyber risk management programs should evolve over time as organizations grow and technology environments change.

Regular maturity assessments help ensure that cyber risk governance remains aligned with business priorities and emerging threats.
